The Firm’s litigation practice has a broad national and international scope. Its litigators have handled significant trials, arbitrations and appeals in more than 25 states in both federal and state courts, in more than half of the United States Courts of Appeals and in the United States Supreme Court. We frequently represent foreign clients in disputes in the United States and United States clients in matters to be resolved in whole or part abroad.


Matters representative of our practice include:

  • In the credit markets crisis, our Firm has represented financial institutions, funds and institutional investors in the array of litigation emerging among counter-parties. Our work in this area includes substantial litigation pre-dating the crisis. Many of the disputes our attorneys have handled present novel issues and situations not contemplated by the documentation of transactions. The Firm’s attorneys have broad experience in matters relating to these markets and, more generally, in economics and finance.
  • We represented a European software developer that makes a leading anti-spyware program — often listed as the most frequently downloaded software in the world. Targets of the anti-spyware software have brought claims under various theories in an attempt to take themselves out of the software’s reach. In one of these cases, we obtained dismissal and an award of attorneys’ fees under a California anti-SLAPP (Strategic Lawsuit Against Public Participation) statute that protects against lawsuits that stifle communication in the public interest, an emerging issue in laws regulating the Internet.
  • The Firm's attorneys has represented the plaintiff in a high-profile art appropriation case applying the fair use defense to a claim of copyright infringement and the case has led to the leading opinion in the fair use area in the Second Circuit.
  • The Firm's attorneys wrote the winning brief in a 5-to-4 decision of the U.S. Supreme Court in a landmark Title VII employment case.
  • Recovered a $13 million arbitration award on behalf of a Dubai commodities trader alleging wrongful liquidation of his account by a futures commission merchant, at the time the largest award from a National Futures Association panel.
  • The Firm's attorneys successfully defended former member of the Board of Directors of the New York Stock Exchange in an arbitration alleging gender discrimination and sexual harassment.
  • The Firm has successfully represented the former owner of the world’s largest international money transmittal company in litigation with the company’s buyers to secure a final payment in the face of breach of warranty claims seeking millions of dollars, but which, in our litigation, were determined to be baseless.
  • The Firm's attorneys represented an international tennis star in an anti-trust claim against the governing men’s tennis organization.
  • The Firm has participated in the representation of Afghan nationals detained at Guantánamo Bay Naval Station as petitioners for writs of habeas corpus, including in bringing one petitioner’s case to a hearing on the merits of the Executive Branch’s claim that the petitioner, arrested in his home, may lawfully be imprisoned pursuant to the congressional authorization for the use of military force.
  • The Firm's attorneys have recovered damages for a Saudi appliance distributor against a leading manufacturer of electrical appliances for wrongful termination of a distributorship agreement.
  • The Firm obtained an ex parte temporary restraining order and preliminary injunctive relief on behalf of a public company to secure its right to convert debt obligations into a controlling interest in the debtor companies (interests valued at over $7,000,000), and supervised ancillary litigation in Italy and the Yukon Territory in Canada.
  • We secured a jury verdict in excess of $4 million on behalf of a European investor based upon actual fraud claims against British and American financial services industry professionals, including tracing the proceeds of the fraud into four separate states.
  • In a case involving a $60+ million music property, the Firm prevailed in the Second Circuit Court of Appeals in a case of first impression, allowing a co-owner of a copyright to pursue its copyright infringement remedies unilaterally, even in the face of a potentially collusive agreement between the defendants and the other co-authors.
  • Recovered a defense verdict in a federal jury trial for a brokerage firm accused of purchasing mortgage-backed securities from another broker/dealer based on inside information.
  • Fully recovered losses suffered by a synagogue which was induced by its broker to invest its funds entirely in mortgage-backed securities.
  • We secured a $4,050,000 jury verdict (including an award of $1,500,000 in punitive damages) in an actual fraudulent conveyance claim on behalf of the principal lenders to a company which sued its senior management after management used fraudulent means to take control of the company’s assets.
  • We have litigated claims on behalf of heirs of Duke Ellington that royalties from copyrights in a portion of Mr. Ellington’s compositions were unlawfully obtained by a music publishing company in exchange for “advances” that constituted fully secured short-term loans. We argued that the agreements violated state laws against usury and were void under common law unconscionability principles.
  • One of the Firm’s attorneys represented a major financial institution in successfully enjoining the sale of petroleum valued at approximately $60 million.
  • The Firm’s attorneys spearheaded the litigation for a major bank in South Africa to recover losses uncovered during a vault balancing exercise totaling approximately $54 million as a result of the dematerialization of physical share certificates and the implementation of STRATE (the licensed South Africa Central Securities Depository for the electronic settlement of financial instruments).
  • One of the Firm’s attorneys functioned as lead litigator for an international petroleum company for over a decade.
  • We have defeated challenges to the appointments of the Administratrix of the Estate of Duke Ellington and the Executrix of the Estate of his son Mercer Ellington in litigation brought in New York and in Florida by other family members seeking to control the Estates.
  • The Firm has developed an expertise in representing clients in connection with unauthorized exploitation of the name and likeness of celebrities. The Firm frequently undertakes trademark enforcement cases on behalf of clients including Duke Ellington’s Estate.
  • We represented one of the largest creditors in a Chapter 11 bankruptcy reorganization in its claims for in excess of $7,000,000 arising from the client’s acquisition of a former subsidiary of the debtor. The claims included demand for an adjustment to the purchase price in accordance with the acquisition agreement and breach of warranty and misrepresentation with regard to the representations made prior to the acquisition.
  • The Firm's attorneys have blocked injunctive relief against a brokerage firm client accused of raiding a competitor’s entire credit derivatives desk, all of whose brokers had contractual non-compete restrictions
  • The Firm's attorneys have successfully defended a broker/dealer and its CEO against a $10 million wrongful liquidation and fraud claim.
  • The Firm's attorneys obtained a permanent injunction enforcing restrictive covenants and non-compete provisions in client’s employment contract with its former President.
  • The Firm's attorneys obtained a defense verdict in federal jury trial in which a broker/dealer client was alleged to have churned its customer’s account, causing it to be liquidated.
  • We represented one of New York’s largest museums in litigation defending substantial breach of contract claims brought by major contractors that had been involved in the $60,000,000+ renovation of the museum’s physical plant.
  • We successfully tried the claims of a minority shareholder in a public company holding convertible preferred stock commonly known as “death-spiral preferred” stock. The outcome of the case was to increase the client’s stake in the company’s common stock from approximately 15% to approximately 50%, with resulting control of the company.
  • We successfully moved, prior to filing an answer, for dismissal of, and alternatively for summary judgment against, defamation claims against an individual client in a case involving public speech rights.
  • We successfully defended at trial a software provider in a multi-million dollar rescission action. The client manufactured one of the country’s leading inventory and warehouse management software products.
  • We represented and achieved a positive settlement for seven citizens of Sweden who were defendants in cases alleging insider trading brought by the United States Securities and Exchange Commission and its enforcement counterpart in Sweden. The case was described in Sweden as the most significant of its kind under Sweden’s relatively new laws regulating securities transactions.
  • The Firm has represented one of the largest enterprises in the United States seeking to bring automobiles manufactured in China to the United States, including representation of its principal, who has a long record of success in this field, in a number of lawsuits involving claims of securities fraud and breach of fiduciary duty, among other issues, and controversies among investors in the enterprise concerning its future direction.
  • The Firm has represented a major insurance company in a number of matters including the investigation of significant fraud by brokerages.
  • We defended the developers of a 250-unit residential real estate development against allegations of Martin Act violations and common law fraud brought by the Attorney General of the State of New York, winning dismissal of those claims by motion.
  • We represented an international fragrance and cosmetics manufacturer against former distributors selling gray market and “knock-off” products, and obtained a temporary restraining order and preliminary injunctive relief to protect the client.
  • We successfully represented the holder of the residual interest in mortgage-backed securities in claims against the financial institutions which acquired those interests, asserting that the valuations used in exercising buy-back rights were grossly understated. In that litigation, we secured a favorable decision from the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York, in a case of first impression, concerning the scope of discovery in federal securities litigation under the Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995.
  • We have served as Special Counsel to a major Hong Kong manufacturing concern to investigate allegations of fraud against members of the management of its partly-owned United States affiliate. Our representation included assisting the client in effecting an efficient change in management and gaining control of the United States affiliate.
  • We obtained injunctive relief for one of the major suppliers of specialty products to the veterinary research community against a former affiliate that was engaging in sales barred by agreements between the two companies.
  • We have represented in litigation one of New York City’s unique venture capital firms investing in Internet companies and new media industries located in New York City, as well as a number of companies in which that firm holds an interest.
  • We have worked with one of the largest law firms in Buenos Aires concerning issues arising out of economic crises in Argentina.
  • We represented an owner of $15,000,000 of real estate in the Tribeca area of New York City in a dispute with co-owners concerning investments made between 1943 and 1992 in various loosely-documented partnerships. The litigation also involved various of the partners’ ventures in real estate development outside New York and manufacturing and service businesses that the partners created with others, using profits from the original real estate holdings. The Firm obtained a settlement at the commencement of trial at a level near the maximum possible recovery.
  • We have represented a former shareholder in one of the nation’s leading manufacturers of specialty brass and bronze fixtures in an arbitration to recover for fraud in the presentation of financial information used to induce our client’s sale of its interest in the company.

  • The Firm's attorneys have obtained reversal of a murder conviction for a client serving a life sentence.
  • The Firm's attorneys successfully argued in a Mississippi federal court that two chapters of the Mississippi Code, dating to the Civil War and dealing with provisional remedies, were unconstitutional.
  • We have represented the guardians of most of the severely mentally disabled persons residing in The Southbury Training School (“STS”), the only residential facility housing the disabled in the State of Connecticut, and a private Foundation established by past and current guardians to ensure the quality of life at the facility. STS has been home to its residents for most of their lives. A central issue in the cases is the belief by many of the guardians that STS is the only viable location for the care of their wards. In one of the cases, the Firm, in tandem with the State of Connecticut, prosecuted to the United States Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit an appeal against the Justice Department challenging a finding in the District Court that the State was in contempt of a 1986 Consent Decree and Judgment relating to the operation of the facility. The Firm presented the argument of first impression that the procedures employed by the Justice Department in winning the contempt order under the Constitutional Rights of Institutionalized Persons Act are not available to seek contempt sanctions in the context of claimed violations of a Consent Decree. In the second case, a class action brought in the name of the wards of the Firm’s clients but funded by private interest groups, we represented the same guardians in an unprecedented request to the District Court that their wards be permitted to opt out of the class. Opting out typically is unavailable in class action civil rights litigation. In excess of 82% of the class members joined the motion to opt out of the class.
